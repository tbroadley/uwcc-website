About Us

UWCC was initiated in Fall 2012 as "University of Waterloo Compost Coalition" and was established by Joshua Jodoin and a team of Environment students. In 2013, the project was passed on to four other students, Kevin Rotseart, Clover Mei, Mike Hager, and Brandon Caron, who continued the research and groundwork for the eventual launch of the program.

In 2014, Brandon, Clover, and Mike established the executive team along with Andres Rodriguez and Monique Yu. They re-launched the project as "University of Waterloo Campus Compost" as the group had evolved significantly since initial conception. To date, our 100% student-run composting operation is perhaps the most unique among all Canadian post-secondary institutions. In 2014 and 2015, UWCC acquired two compost drums (affectionately referred to as compost cows) from Actium Composting and initiated the first year of operations, which ultimately resulted in success and nearly 2,200 pounds of diverted organic waste. Within this pilot year, UWCC also joined Sustainable Campus Initiative as an ongoing project to help further establish the group's ties and voice support for all on-campus green initiatives.

UWCC has shown tremendous growth over the years. Currently, UWCC provides composting services to over 25 locations in faculty offices, food services, and coffee shops as well as various events. We have also collaborated with orientation leaders by providing services to selected events during Orientation Week. UWCC is continually seeking the best way to maximize our capacity each term, and we appreciate the help of all volunteers who have helped us come this far. In recent terms, we have also been shifting our focus towards education and advocacy initiatives to spread awareness about composting.

Our Teams

Operations

The operations team is a group of dedicated volunteers who have accepted the responsibility of transforming our ideas into actions. These volunteers ensure that compost is collected 2-3 times a week from our locations as well as record observations and data regarding the quantity and quality of material in our bins. In addition, they also transfer organic material and shredded paper into our compost cows and monitor the progress of the compost process by regularly checking temperatures and turning the cows. Since the operations team is the most dominant aspect of our activities on-campus, the growth of our organization heavily depends on this group of passionate volunteers. Aside from our day-to-day operations, this team is also responsible for the maintenance of an appropriate carbon-nitrogen ratio as well as data management.

Marketing

The marketing team collaborates closely with the operations team as well as the organization's executive team to facilitate the growth of UWCC through the engagement of various stakeholders. The purpose of the marketing team is to generate public interest in waste reduction through composting, educate and advocate for the benefits of composting, and promote other UW student initiatives toward sustainability as well. Through the use of social media, posters, awareness campaigns, events, and collaborations with other groups, the team works to increase the visibility of the organization on-campus.

Membership

In addition to the operations and marketing teams, UWCC also has a small membership team that is responsible for overseeing successful volunteer recruitment, management, and retention as well as other human resources initiatives. Currently, the membership team only consists of executive members, but as we grow, there may be a need for a larger membership team for UWCC.
